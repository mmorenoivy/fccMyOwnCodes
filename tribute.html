Sheryl Sandberg

Woman, Work and Will to Succeed

Sheryl Sandberg is the chief operating officer of Facebook and best-selling author of Lean In: Women, Work, and the Will to Lead.

Here's a time line of Sheryl Sandberg's life:

  • Born 1969
    Born in Washington, D.C.
  • Moved to Miami, Florida 1971
    Moved with her family to North Miami Beach, Florida
  • Graduated High School 1987
    At North Miami Beach Senior High School,
    Sandberg was in the National Honor Society
    and graduated ninth in her class,
    with a 4.6 GPA.
  • Degree in Economics 1991 - 1993
    Graduated summa cum laude, majored in Economics. At the same year Lawrence Summers asked Sandberg to be one of his research assistants.
  • Got Married 1991
    Sandberg married Washington businessman Brian Kraff
  • Divorced 1992
    Divorced Brian Kraff
  • M.B.A 1995
    Enrolled at Harvard Business School, attaining
    her M.B.A and graduated with distinction
  • Chief of Staff 1999 - 2001
    Sandberg became chief of staff of Lawrence Summers during Clinton's Administration
  • Vice-President 2001 - 2008
    Sandberg accepted Google's offer as Vice President and
    described Google's mission as "to make the world's information freely available"
  • Got Married 2004
    Sandberg married Dave Goldberg, a Yahoo! executive
  • Chief Operating Officer 2008 - present
    Joins Facebook as COO
  • Board Director, Facebook 2012
    Sandberg became the first female member of the company's board of directors
  • Billionaire's Club 2014
    Made her way to billionaire's list in early 2014
  • Goldberg passes 2015
    Dave Goldberg died due to head trauma. Sandberg wrote Lean In

"Dave was my rock. When I got upset, he stayed calm. When I was worried, he said it would be ok. When I wasn't sure what to do, he figured it out. He was completely dedicated to his children in every way – and their strength these past few days is the best sign I could have that Dave is still here with us in spirit. . .Things will never be the same – but the world is better for the years my beloved husband lived."

Sheryl Sandberg, May 1, 2015
